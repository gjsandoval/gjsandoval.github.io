Grecio J. Sandoval

I am an Assistant Research Professor of Biostatistics and Bioinformatics at the Biostatistics Center at the Milken Institute School of Public Health at the George Washington University. I work as a statistician for the Eunice Kennedy Shriver NICHD Maternal Fetal Medicine Units (MFMU) Network and for the Prenatal Genetic Diagnosis by Genomic Sequencing study. Prior to joining the Biostatistics Center, I worked for 7 years as a biostatistician at PROMETRIKA, LLC, a contract research organization located in Cambridge, Massachusetts. Â 

I received a BS in Mathematics from the University of Texas at Austin in 2007 and a MA in Biostatistics from Boston University in 2009. Under the direction of Drs. Ionut Bebu and John Lachin, I received a PhD in Biostatistics from the George Washington University in 2021. My research interests include the design of clinical trial and epidemiological studies, cost-effectiveness analysis, and healthcare resource utilization.
